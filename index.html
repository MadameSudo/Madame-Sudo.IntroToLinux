Intro to Linux

What is Linux?

Linux is a free and open-source operating system that can be used on a variety of devices, from desktop computers to smartphones to servers.

Why use Linux?

  • It's free and open-source
  • It's highly customizable
  • It's secure and stable
  • It has a large and supportive community

Getting started with Linux

To get started with Linux, you can download a distribution such as Ubuntu, Fedora, or Debian and install it on your computer. There are also many resources available online to help you learn more about using and customizing Linux.

Different Linux distributions you could experiment with!

There are many different Linux distributions, each with its own strengths and weaknesses. Some popular options include:

  • Linux Mint - a beginner-friendly distribution that comes with many useful applications pre-installed.
  • Arch Linux - a lightweight and highly customizable distribution that is popular among advanced users.
  • Red Hat Enterprise Linux - a commercial distribution that is popular in corporate environments.
  • CentOS - a free, community-driven distribution that is based on Red Hat Enterprise Linux.
  • Manjaro - a user-friendly distribution that is based on Arch Linux.
  • OpenSUSE - a stable and easy-to-use distribution that is popular among developers.
